Plant Directory

Pothos

An easy to care for, but poisonous, this trailing plant called Devil's Ivy is beginner friendly. When it thrives it can grow to many feet in length and can easily be propagated by cutting the plant by the nodes. Easy to keep and beautiful, owners must be careful that their pets do not make a snack of it as it can be somewhat toxic.

Varieties

  • Golden
  • Jade
  • Manjula
  • Marble Queen
  • N'joy

Peperomia

A very large plant family comprised of over 1,000 species, beginner friendly and tropical. The leaves of this plant are typically waxy and firm, it is a busy plant that can spread out in many directions and take over the space. It is tolerant of neglect and is easy to keep in low light areas. There are many varieties some more decorative than others.

Varieties

  • Pincushion
  • Raindrop
  • Trailing
  • Watermelon

Ficus

These plants are frequently used in offices with low light, and are also known to improve air quality. With many different varieties to liven up modern architecture, the rubber plant can grow to 70' natively in tropical climates outdoors. Inside, it has a much smaller profile though is still very prolific in its growth rate due to how resistant it is to neglect. It can be toxic to animals, and the white sap it produces when injured has been known to irritate human skin. It enjoys being rootbound.

Varieties

  • Creeping
  • Fiddle Leaf
  • Ruby
  • Sophia
  • Tineke

Philodendron

Able to survive in all sorts of lighting and watering conditions, these plants adapt to indoor life well. These plants vine, or trail, so do well hung up, though a trellis can be erected to encourage their growth upwards. There have been many new varieties added to the indoor plant market over the past couple of years which have made them quite popular, and many of these varieties are quite striking. They are hard to over-water and are good for beginners, though some can be quite expensive.

Varieties

  • Blushing
  • Cordatum
  • Micans
  • Pink Princess
  • White Princess

Syngonium

Flowering tropical plant family with many distinct and beautiful varities for indoor decoration. Technically a vining plant, the growth of these leaves is from a single stem, each leaf producing the next in a long trail. In nature, they would cling to and grow up the bark of large trees, in the home they must be supported by tomato tresslis or allowed otherwise propped up lest they tumble free of their pot. Many gorgeous varieties exist, but they require high humidity and more direct care.

Varieties

  • Albovirens
  • Berry Allusion
  • Confetti
  • Pink
  • White Butterfly

Marantaceae

Also known as arrowroots, these plants are known for their movement throughout the day as they follow the light. Due to their high movement and how the plant may stretch its leaves upwards, these plants are also known as prayer plants. They require high humidity and are known for being very fussy and not beginner friendly. They are easy to overwater, but absolutely loathe being dried out, so they are not recommended for new growers. There are many species and varieties to choose from when you embark upon the journey of growing these plants.

Varieties

  • Calathea
  • Ctenanthe
  • Goeppertia
  • Maranta
  • Stromanthe

Monstera

These large tropical plants typically grow around large trees, and have become very popular in the plant trade. Growing enormous leaves in the wild, allowing these plants to flourish in the home properly can be difficult, but even though they come from tropical conditions, they are easy to care for indoors for beginners, but quite difficult to master. Their leaves form fenestrations, or holes, which either protect them from high hurricane winds or allow light to filter to smaller leaves below, but these leaves are considered extremely beautiful in the modern plant trade. There are several expensive varieties, some fetching thousands of dollars on the market.

Varieties

  • Adansonii
  • Borsigniana
  • Deliciosa
  • Obliqua
  • Variegata
